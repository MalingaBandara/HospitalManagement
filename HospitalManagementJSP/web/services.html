Electromyography

The use of an instrument, which records electric currents generated in an active muscle. A concentric needle electrode is inserted into voluntary muscle and the amplified recording is viewed on an oscilloscope and heard through a speaker.

M.R.I Scanning

M.R.I. means Magnetic resonance imaging which is a type of scan that uses strong magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed images of the inside of the body.

holter Monitoring

Continuous recording of one or two ECG leads may be obtained by attaching them to a small portable tape recorder. This is useful in detecting transient episodes of arrhythmia or iscemia (heart diseases), which seldom occurs in 12 lead ECG recordings.
